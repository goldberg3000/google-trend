Kristin Crowley - Wikipedia

Kristin Crowley is the chief of the Los Angeles Fire Department (LAFD), having assumed the role in 2022. She is notable for being the first female fire chief in Los Angeles, following her predecessor Ralph Terrazas, who served from 2014 to 2022. Crowley began her career with the LAFD after passing the firefighters' exam in 1998, where she ranked in the top 50 out of over 16,000 candidates. Since joining the department in 2000, she has held numerous positions, including firefighter, paramedic, engineer, fire inspector, captain, battalion chief, assistant chief, fire marshal, and deputy chief. In 2016, she made history as Los Angeles' first female fire marshal. As chief, Crowley has prioritized the safety, health, and well-being of firefighters and has committed to fostering a work environment free from harassment, discrimination, and hazing. She has also emphasized the importance of diversity within the department to better serve the diverse population of Los Angeles. In addition to her role as fire chief, Crowley was appointed to the Homeland Security Advisory Council in 2023, becoming only the second fire chief to hold that position. She has been active in community events, serving as a grand marshal at Los Angeles Pride in June 2024. Crowley has also been vocal about budgetary concerns, criticizing an $18 million cut to the LAFD's budget in December 2024, which she argued would negatively affect firefighting responses. She has been involved in overseeing the department's response to the Southern California wildfires in January 2025. Outside of her professional life, Crowley is married and has three children.
